Feature Extraction Pipeline

🎵

HCQT — Harmonic CQT

Stacks 6 harmonic CQT layers (h=1…6). All harmonics of a pitch appear at the same bin, creating vertical stripes. 180 bins, 36 bins/octave, 10 ms hop.

📊

VAD Features

3 handcrafted per-frame scalars: RMS energy, spectral flatness (Wiener entropy), zero-crossing rate. Computed causally with ring buffer at inference.

🏷️

F0 Labels

Fast path: librosa.yin (~0.07 s/clip). Accurate path: librosa.pyin (~7.5 s/clip). Range C2–C7. Gaussian soft-labels (σ=2 bins) for pitch training.

🧠

Singer-Level Splits

Train/val/test split by singer_id, not clip. Prevents the model from memorising a singer's voice. Typical 80/10/10 ratio.

⚡

Causal Architecture

All convolutions and GRUs look only at past frames. Enables real-time streaming: feed one 10 ms frame at a time and carry GRU hidden state.

🎯

Focal Loss

Breath (~3–8% positive) and onset (~8–15% positive) frames are rare. Focal loss (γ=2) down-weights easy negatives so the model learns the rare class.

🔮

Viterbi Post-Processing

HMM with 181 states (180 voiced bins + 1 unvoiced). Gaussian pitch transition (σ=2 bins). Applied after argmax decoding to smooth pitch tracks.

🌐

Pitch Augmentation

±3 semitone pitch-shift on training clips. HCQT bins shift by an integer offset, so no re-extraction needed — just bin-wise circular shift.

Dataset Split Strategy

Splits are created by singer ID, not by clip. This ensures the model sees each singer in only one partition — preventing it from memorising voice timbre rather than learning pitch structure.

🎓

Train — 80%

~8,400 clips across all three datasets. Pitch-shift augmentation ±3 semitones applied here only.

🔬

Validation — 10%

~1,050 clips. Model checkpoint saved when best RPA (pitch) or best F1 (VAD/Breath/Onset) is achieved.

🏁

Test — 10%

~1,050 clips. Only touched for the final evaluation reported in the Results tab.
