Motivation Why an AI Vocal Coach?
🎤

The Problem

Most people who sing — in the shower, car, or at karaoke — have no access to feedback. Professional vocal coaches are expensive and inaccessible.

🎯

Target User

Casual beginner singers with no musical training. They want honest, actionable feedback — not scoring or judging — to improve over time.

🧠

Our Definition of "Vocal Coach"

A system that listens, identifies measurable voice patterns (pitch, breath, technique), and returns specific, actionable coaching guidance.

📱

Why AI?

ML models can analyze pitch accuracy, breath control, and vocal technique in real-time from a phone or laptop — no expensive hardware required.

System Goal

Build a web app that takes a short audio recording and returns pitch accuracy analysis, breathing feedback, and technique coaching — running entirely on CPU, no musical expertise required from the user.

2

Preprocessing

  • 16 kHz mono resample
  • 256 ms frames, 50% overlap
  • HCQT features
  • 6 harmonics × 360 bins
  • Log-mel (128 bands)
  • Peak normalization
▶
3

Model

  • Model A — Custom HCQT+GRU
  • ~256K params · 1 MB
  • Outputs: VAD, pitch, onset,
    breath, technique tags
  • NanoPitch — CNN guard
  • 332K params · noise veto
  • pyin — DSP f0 baseline
▶
4

Post-processing

  • Hybrid f0/VAD decision
  • NanoPitch veto on noise
  • Task-aware evaluators
  • f0 smoothing
  • Note segmentation
  • Validity gate (blocks noise)
▶
5

Feedback to User

  • Pitch lane visualization
  • Issue markers on timeline
  • Coaching cards + scores
  • Technique feedback
  • Exercise suggestions

Evaluation Metrics, Why They Matter, and Results

Metric 1 False-Voiced Rate (FVR)

A coaching app that hallucinates singing on background noise gives meaningless feedback. FVR = % of frames wrongly called "voiced" on non-singing audio. Lower is better.

Metric 2 Pitch Jump Rate

Octave/semitone discontinuities in f0 produce incoherent pitch feedback. The hybrid uses pyin for clean f0 on diagnostic tasks. Lower is better.
